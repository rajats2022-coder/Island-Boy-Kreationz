What he needs ready

The shoot only works if the food, truck, owner, and registration flow are prepared before the camera arrives.

Food

  • One hero oxtail plate or bowl.
  • One wing meal or wing bowl.
  • One chopped chicken bowl.
  • One curry chicken plate.
  • One shrimp or salmon bowl.
  • One tray setup with rice, cabbage, mac, yams, cornbread, cake, and drinks if available.

Truck and set

  • Truck washed and parked where the logo and window are visible.
  • Clean counter, utensils, gloves, napkins, bags, and branded items.
  • Menu board or printed menu nearby.
  • Small table for plates and tray shots.
  • QR code sign for registration.

Owner

  • Solid shirt or apron, no tiny patterns.
  • 3-5 story points, not memorized lines.
  • Ready to answer in full sentences.
  • One direct-to-camera invite.
  • One sincere thank-you to the community.

Production

  • Phone/camera shooting vertical 9:16.
  • Lav mic or wireless mic.
  • Tripod or gimbal.
  • Small light or shade plan.
  • Extra batteries, wipes, backup storage, and customer permission plan.

Interview and hook clips

The research points to raw, useful, owner-led clips: real process, real people, real questions, and a clear CTA.

Interview setup

Film him near the truck, not in a blank room. The truck, food, and hands working should prove the story while he talks.

  • A-cam: chest-up 45 degrees, owner looking at interviewer.
  • B-roll: hands plating, truck window, line, sauce pour.
  • Direct lens only for the event invite and thank-you.
  • Best questions: "What does seven years mean to you?", "Why give food away?", "What dish tells your story?", "What is the hardest part of running a food truck?", "What should someone try first?"

Hook/stitch structure

Each short clip should move fast: no logo intro, no long setup, no vague caption.

0-1 secStrongest sensory food visual.
1-3 sec5-8 word hook text.
3-8 secProof: food, crowd, prep, or reaction.
8-15 secEvent details: date, location, free food.
15-20 secOne CTA: register or comment PLATE.
