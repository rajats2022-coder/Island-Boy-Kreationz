Food B-roll plan

Three B-roll angles for each food.

Shot rule for every food item

  • Angle 1: close-up motion, like sauce pouring, steam, cutting, scooping, or tray opening.
  • Angle 2: finished plate from overhead or 45 degrees so people see the full meal.
  • Angle 3: action shot, like fork pull, handoff at the truck window, or first bite.

Oxtails

  • Close-up: gravy pour over rice.
  • Plate: finished meal with sides.
  • Action: fork pull to show tenderness.

Wings

  • Close-up: sauce toss or sauce brush.
  • Plate: wings with mac and cabbage.
  • Action: first-bite reaction.

Chicken bowls

  • Close-up: rice and chicken build.
  • Plate: final bowl reveal.
  • Action: fork scoop toward camera.

Curry chicken

  • Close-up: steam or curry pour.
  • Plate: side angle of the finished meal.
  • Action: fork pull or plate handoff.

Shrimp or salmon

  • Close-up: protein placed on rice.
  • Plate: colorful overhead bowl shot.
  • Action: fork scoop toward camera.

Sides and trays

  • Close-up: mac scoop, yams spoon pull, cabbage, or rice.
  • Plate: full tray or side spread.
  • Action: tray opening or serving spoon shot.
Production focus Each food item will be filmed from three useful angles so the footage can be reused throughout the month.

Interview and hook clips

Short clips designed to catch attention.

Three interview-style questions per shoot

  • Question 1: the anniversary, the community, and why he is giving back.
  • Question 2: the food, his favorite plate, or the weekly special.
  • Question 3: running a food truck, business lessons, or advice for other owners.

Three hook clips per shoot

  • A hook clip is a quick couple-second clip made to stop the scroll.
  • It starts with food moving, a funny line, or a bold message.
  • Then we give one quick action: register, save, try the special, or leave a review.

Example funny messages

  • "Do not watch this hungry."
  • "If you see the yellow truck, make the turn."
  • "This plate does not need a filter."
  • "The line moves faster when you register."
  • "Try the special, then tell Google about it."
Weekly package Three interview answers, three hook clips, food B-roll, one weekly special, one schedule post, and one Google review reminder.

What needs to be ready

Shoot checklist before we film.

Food ready

  • Oxtail plate or bowl.
  • Wing meal or wing bowl.
  • Chicken bowl or curry chicken plate.
  • Shrimp or salmon bowl if available.
  • Sides: mac, cabbage, yams, rice, cornbread.
  • One tray setup if catering/event trays are available.
  • One weekly special picked before filming.

Truck ready

  • Truck washed and parked in good light.
  • Window and logo visible.
  • Counter area clean.
  • Menu or event sign visible.
  • Registration QR code printed or ready on screen.
  • Google review QR code printed or ready on screen.

Owner ready

  • Solid shirt or apron.
  • Ready to talk naturally, not memorize.
  • Prepared to answer in full sentences.
  • One thank-you message for the community.
  • One direct invitation to the event.

Event info ready

  • Exact date and time.
  • Exact location.
  • What food will be served.
  • This week's special.
  • Registration link.
  • Food limit or "while supplies last" language.

After he approves this plan

  • We will build the detailed shoot schedule.
  • We will write the exact scripts and captions.
  • We will make the registration page or form.
  • We will create the Google review QR code/sign.
  • We will create a final shot checklist for shoot day.
